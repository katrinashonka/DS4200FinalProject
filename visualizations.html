Data Visualizations

Below are our 5 data visualizations. Some will have interactive elements to help you explore the data in depth. Explanations of design decisions will be documented separately.

1. Stacked Bar Chart of Yes/No Symptoms (D3.js)

This chart shows the frequency of panic attack symptoms reported as Yes or No. The categories are sorted by how commonly they are reported.

2. Grouped Bar Chart: Therapy/Medication Impact

This chart compares different combinations of therapy and medication on panic severity levels (Low, Medium, High).

3. 3. Normalized Stacked Bar Chart (Altair) - Panic Severity

An interactive normalized stacked bar chart to visualize the relationship between panic attack severity and symptoms and lifestyle factors.

This visualization reveals that both lifestyle factors and physical symptoms are closely tied to the severity of stres -induced panic attacks. Poor lifestyle habits—particularly low sleep and high alcohol consumption—are associated with a greater proportion of high-severity panic episodes. Similarly, symptoms like chest pain, sweating, and dizziness tend to occur more frequently during severe attacks. Together, this suggests that both preventative habits and symptom awareness play critical roles in understanding and managing panic severity.

4. Stacked Bar Chart (Altair) - Panic Severity

An interactive stacked bar chart to visualize the relationship between panic attack severity and symptoms and lifestyle factors.

5. Dot Plot With Filter (Altair) - Sleep Hours vs Panic Score

This chart allows you to interactively explore the effect of sleep on the severity of panic attacks.

6. Heatmap (Python) - Lifestyle Factors vs. Panic Severity

A correlation heatmap to explore relationships between continuous variables such as caffeine intake, sleep, alcohol intake, heart rate, and exercise frequency with panic severity score.

7. Correlation Heatmap (Python) - Panic Score vs. Lifestyle

This heatmap focuses specifically on how Panic Score is correlated with lifestyle variables like caffeine, exercise, alcohol, and smoking. It helps highlight the most potentially influential factors.
