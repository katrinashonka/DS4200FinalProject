Analyzing Panic Attack Data: Lifestyle Influences, Potential Triggers, and Symptoms

Panic attacks can be frightening and overwhelming. By studying the factors behind them— including triggers, lifestyle habits, and symptom patterns—we hope to shed light on prevention and improved treatment strategies for those affected by anxiety disorders.

Introduction

Welcome to our project analyzing panic attack data. This website explores how lifestyle factors, potential triggers, and symptoms may relate to panic attacks. Our tasks include:

  • Investigating lifestyle influences (caffeine, sleep, exercise, etc.)
  • Examining potential triggers (stress, phobias, social anxiety, etc.)
  • Exploring symptom severity and frequency
  • Creating interactive visualizations to help interpret the data

Through these tasks, we aim to discover patterns and insights that can inform better prevention and treatment strategies.

About the Data

Our dataset, “Panic Attack Dataset”, is publicly available on Kaggle and can be downloaded as a 94.66 kB CSV file. It contains 1,200 rows and 21 attributes, including:

    Demographics

  • ID – Unique identifier
  • Age – Age of the individual
  • Gender – Male, Female, Non-binary

    • Panic Attack Characteristics

  • Panic_Attack_Frequency – Number of panic attacks per month
  • Duration_Minutes – Average duration of panic attack (minutes)
  • Trigger – Main trigger (stress, social anxiety, phobia, etc.)
  • Panic_Score – Severity score (1-10)

    • Symptoms

  • Heart_Rate – Heart rate during a panic attack (bpm)
  • Sweating – Yes/No
  • Shortness_of_Breath – Yes/No
  • Dizziness – Yes/No
  • Chest_Pain – Yes/No
  • Trembling – Yes/No

    • Lifestyle Factors

  • Medical_History – Pre-existing conditions (Anxiety, Depression, PTSD)
  • Medication – Taking medication for anxiety (Yes/No)
  • Caffeine_Intake – Cups of coffee/tea per day
  • Exercise_Frequency – Days of exercise per week
  • Sleep_Hours – Average sleep hours per night
  • Alcohol_Consumption – Drinks per week
  • Smoking – Smoker (Yes/No)
  • Therapy – Attending therapy (Yes/No)
